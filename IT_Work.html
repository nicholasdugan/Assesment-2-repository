IT Work

Some analysis of IT Professionals from Web Sources.

Summaries:


A Day in the Life of an IT Support Specialist

Dawood Ahmed is an IT Support Specialist which means he is the first point of contact for anyone experiencing issues relating to IT within his business. His business has a staff of over 2500 so issues for him relate to anything from frozen computers to dealing with Cloud issues. To be at the point he is at Dawood has had to complete a diploma as well as a certificate III and IV in IT Networking.



A Day in the Life of an IT Pro: IT Manager

Johnathan Dalia is the IT manager for Langer Transportation which is a trucking company based in America. His role comprises of lots of different tasks some of the roles in which he performs are maintaining data links between trucks and offices, maintaining servers and data recovery systems as well as trouble shooting any issues that may present themselves and ensuring that the team under him is operating effectively. To reach where he is Johnathan studied a Bachelors degree in Network Communication and in order to progress further he completed a masters in IT Administration and Security.



A Day in the Life of a Software Engineer

Forest Knight is a full development software working multiple different roles in the creation of software and websites. Currently he is creating an enterprise software for clients and is involved with leading the team of all developers however he does work in front end development, backend work primarily in java as well as doing software testing and analysis.

Day in the Life of a Web Designer | Flux

Ran Segall is a web designer who works in the creation of websites however specialises in front end design and user experience development. He runs is own business called Flux which is a design studio as well as a media company teaching how to effectively work in web design, he runs this through Youtube as well as his own website and courses. To reach this capability he studied graphic design and visual communication and university and has also worked in the design industry before he studied which has given him lots of experience.



Day In The Life Web Designer - probably not what you think

Kay Cinho is a web designer that does freelance work. He works from home in general however he also will pay to go into co-working spaces for something different, and it also offers a place to meet clients if they aren't local or don't have an office. His routine is to confirm the schedule for the day and see if any issues have arisen for clients that need his attention. He attempts to have all client meetings on a single day to cut down on traffic frustrations.



A day in the life of a UX Designer in San Francisco (but forreal)

Alexa Herasimchuk is a Product Manager/UX Designer at Zendesk. Her work day can start at home with making calls before leaving for the office. Due to her position most of her day are taken up with meetings as this allows her to communicate everything with all the different teams to make sure everything is going appropriately. As her focus is on the user experience, she works closely with the software engineering team as there may be technical reasons that a specific design cannot be used. Alexa studied Graphic Design before moving into what she is currently doing.



Day in the Life of a Graphic Designer

Paul Zana is a Graphic Designer, as well as providing design courses for people. He shows that after waking he will start his day with planning and then sketching certain elements of design. He shows that the work he does at home is the UI/UX side of things. After sketching he will work on his computer to mock up the designs from his sketches in Figma. After having lunch he will then head into the office. In the office it shows him doing other design, in this case sketching mock-ups for a logo. It also has him showing the updating the workflow management (in this case a board with sticky notes) to say he has done the work on the UI/UX refinement. He then heads home, takes a client call and continues to work on refining sketches while watching things to relax.



Day in the Life of a Japanese Game Programmer

Masa is a game programmer for a BANDAI NAMCO Studios in Tokyo. When entering the office he swipes a digital timecard starting his work for the day. The start of his day is checking email and the internal company social network for any updates. It also covers the facilities that the company offers within the office, including a gaming section with consoles, and the break room area. Most of Masa's time in the office is spent coding with a break for lunch and a daily meeting. They are also able to go take a nap in a dedicated nap area if required. Before finishing he will make sure he hasn't missed any notifications and log all the work he did for the day.



Day in the Life of a Software Engineer (Los Angeles)

Jed Calaug is a Software Engineer working as a Front End Engineer. Due to the company having no headquarters everyone reports in online. He usually only takes one call a day which is the morning standup call at 8am where everyone reports what they did the previous day. The reason for lack of calls is they are trusted to do the work they are required and put the hours they are meant to each day. After taking the call he will then code for the rest of the day, with a total of 8 hours a day working.
